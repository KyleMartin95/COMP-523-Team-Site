System Testing

Since we are currently in development, and only have access to sample data, the majority of our test cases involve simulating UX and backend communication. We expect our UX to operate with similar ease to Google Maps or other routing applications. Besides preventing any user confusion, possible breaks to avoid are centrally focused around entered locations returning incorrect results on the map or a route that is irrelevant. Lastly, the most important testing would be for our routing algorithm, in which case an undesirable route or a non-optimal route is returned when there exists a more optimal chain of routes, for example.

Freelance Testing

After a sample database is further collected and we shift focus from web to mobile development, sets UNC students and colleagues of our client might be asked to test the application given a list of sample paramaters. To test the different groups of users, we will ask UNC students to plan a trip ahead of time as if they were visiting a location as a tourist to test usability, and colleagues of our client in Nicaragua to test practicality.
